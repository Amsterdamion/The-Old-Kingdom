## Welcome to the Old Kingdom Adventurer, we are glad you are here with us. 
Welcome! This is my first modlist and is still in beta, thank you for testing it and being part of our journey into the darkest reaches of Tamriel. As you can guess, this is very different than ElderTeej in design, aesthetic, and philosphy. This list was in development alongside Numidium and has quite a few features that overlap such as Campfire, Andrealletius' Jobs Overhaul (AJO), and Settlement Builder.

## Why play this versus Numidium?
The main diversion is in gameplay design here. The Old Kingdom utilizes a basis of Nirn's Chosen to bring back the class system of previous Elder Scrolls titles along with Start Me Up to provide enough points to put one into most of your starting skills. The idea is to introduce more potent player choice in the character you build and life you choose to lead in the Old Kingdom. Here, life is harsh, death is swift. The perks you pick will make you stronger, but will also have consequences as well. You must live off the land, utilizing Campfire to improve your skills at survival. The farthest reaches of Northern Skyrim are treacherous frigid planes of snow and ice, ensure you are prepared for the environment to be as great a foe as the bandits hiding around the next dark corner. Experience is gained by what you do, not how you do it. Your skills will progress as you utilize your natural talents, but the only way to level up is by accomplishing tasks, exploring, reading, fighting, or training. Experience is earned in The Old Kingdom. Nothing will be handed to you, and you will be all the more rewarded for your efforts. Make no mistake dear Adventurer, you will die, and Skyrim's dark heart will eventually claim your soul. It is how you face your death that determines the hero you have become. Please reference the Old Kingdom Gameplay Guide for a full breakdown of features and tutorials.

## Features
- Classic RPG progression – As mentioned above, here in The Old Kingdom, your stats still improve through usage. This now extends to the reserves of your HP, Stamina, and Magicka. Now if you are a mighty warrior who is clad in heavy armor, your health and stamina will increase from all of the frontline damage you will be taking and the onslaught you will unleash in return. A mage conversely will see the largest gains in their magicka reserves rather than health or stamina as they will be doing their best to avoid damage at all costs. Rogues similarly will see the largest gains in Stamina and subsequently carry weight for all their ill gotten goods. 

- No Auto or Quick Saving - Like the RPGs of Olde there is as little handholding as possible which extends to how we handle saves. There are no auto saves or quick saving of any kind and you must rely on making hard saves for your progress. We also use Respawn - Souls Like Death Alternative to send you back to the Temple of Kynareth after any misadventures. Inventory intact, save for your gold which is entirely lost upon 'death' you must return to the place of your demise to regain your Septims and if you die on the return trip, that gold is lost forever.
  
- Lethal, modernized combat – StancesNG with the SIGMA suite of First Person anims and the Nordic/Desert/Imperium pack for Third Person anims, Valhalla Combat, Precision, SCAR, Locational Damage, and TK Dodge create stamina-driven, positional fights where mistakes are fatal. Our combat vision is closer to Elden Ring than God of War or For Honor. This is NOT a power fantasy list. You will die. A lot. That is okay, and part of the intended gameplay loop. 

- Low-magic, high-cost spellcasting – Thaumaturgy, Stormcrown, Spell Research, Mysticism, and Apocalypse form the foundations for magic in the Old Kingdom. Here you must study, practice, and delve into the mysteries of Aetherius to fully unlock your potential as a spell caster. 

- Harsh survival & world pressure – Starfrost, Survival Mode Improved, Campfire, and Realistic Baggage enforce planning, scarcity, and risk. You must plan your excursions into the wild forests and tundras of Skyrims harsh and rugged landscape carefully, the land is as apt to kill you as the denizens within it.

- Reactive enemies and factions – Lawless, Madmen - A Forsworn Overhaul, The Dragon Cult, Rogues 'n Raiders, and Hand Placed Enemies (including ambushes) ensure enemies fight intelligently and factions feel distinct. This is a harsh country, and the people in it are more than willing to kill you if you get in their way. Plan accordingly Adventurer...

- Grounded economy overhaul – The Gilded Road, C.O.I.N. along with M.I.N.T., Dynamic Pricing Framework, and Economy Overhaul and Speechcraft Improvements curb gold inflation and exploit loops but improving rewards and progression with a more organic economy. The land is war torn, supply lines are under siege, and Belethor is still a sleazy little man, but he has good stock for survivalists now.

- Old-world visual grit – Sentinel, Immersive Armors, extensive PBR retextures, and Community Shaders deliver worn, medieval realism over Skyrim's dated depiction of a Nordic Fantasy. 

- Immersive starts and roleplay focus – Alternate Perspective, Why I Came to Skyrim, and Roleplaying in Skyrim systems emphasize identity over the destiny of the Dragonborn.

- Semi-Deleveled, dangerous exploration – Encounter Zones Unlocked, Dungeons – Revisited, and creature overhauls make the world hostile regardless of player level.

### Pre-installation
- Download and install the [Visual C++ x64 Redistributables](https://aka.ms/vc14/vc_redist.x64.exe)
- Download and install the [.NET 9.0 SDK](https://dotnet.microsoft.com/en-us/download/dotnet/9.0)
- Disable OneDrive and any other programs that hook into user file areas
- Do a full reinstall of Skyrim anywhere other than Program Files, but **DO NOT** verify the files in Steam
- Turn off [automated updates for Skyrim in Steam](https://help.steampowered.com/en/faqs/view/71AB-698D-57EB-178C#disable)
- Run your-newly reinstalled Skyrim once through Steam and download all the AE DLC when it prompts you; do not tab-out during this process
### Wabbajack
- Download [Wabbajack](https://github.com/wabbajack-tools/wabbajack/releases/latest/download/Wabbajack.exe) and extract it somewhere that's not a Windows-protected directory (i.e. Downloads, Documents, or Program Files)
- Run Wabbajack.exe
- Tick "show non featured lists"
- Search for The Old Kingdom and click on it
- Press install

### Post-installation
- Enter the folder where you installed the list and run ModOrganizer2.exe
- Ensure that "The Old Kingdom" is selected on the upper-right-hand corner of Mod Organizer 2 and press "Run" to load the game; to play this modlist, you must always launch SSE through MO2
- Enjoy the game!
